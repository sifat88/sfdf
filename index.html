§ 01

The question every founder faces.

When should a founder bootstrap, and when should they raise?

Founders rarely answer this with a structured method. Most pattern-match to recent stories, copy peers, or default to the first available capital. The Framework replaces that habit with a directional reading on fifteen factors that, together, account for most of the decision.

        § 03

        How the scoring works.

        Four steps. No black box. The math is shown so you can defend, contest, or ignore the reading on its merits.

        i.

        Score

        Each factor receives a 1–5 score from the founder, based on self-assessment against the anchor descriptions.

        ii.

        Centre & sign

        Each score is centred (raw minus 3) and signed by direction. Equity-favourable factors contribute positively; bootstrap-favourable factors contribute negatively.

        iii.

        Aggregate

        Within each dimension, factor contributions are averaged. Dimension averages are then weighted (Founder 0.25, Venture 0.30, Market 0.20, Strategic 0.25) to produce the Net Score.

        iv.

        Read the zone

        The Net Score maps to one of five zones along the −2.0 to +2.0 spectrum: Strong Bootstrap, Bootstrap-leaning, Tension, Equity-leaning, Strong Equity.

        The formula
        Net Score  =  (weighted Equity-pull)  −  (weighted Bootstrap-pull)
        § 04

        The Framework stands on six anchors.

        Every factor in the Canvas is tagged to a specific source from the systematic literature review. The framework as a whole rests on these foundations.

        Agency Theory
        Jensen & Meckling (1976)

        Why control and dilution matter. Equity creates principal-agent tensions between founders and external investors.

        Resource Dependence
        Pfeffer & Salancik (1978)

        Why founders accept dilution. External investors offer scarce resources that bootstrapping cannot provide.

        Financial Bootstrapping
        Winborg & Landström (2001)

        How founders self-finance: customer pre-payments, factoring, grants, deferred compensation.

        Founder Choice
        Wasserman (2017)

        The throne vs. the kingdom. Risk tolerance, control, and growth ambition shape the financing path.

        VC Contracting
        Kaplan & Strömberg (2003)

        Empirical anchors for venture and market factors. Capital intensity and traction drive contract terms.

        Value-Add Beyond Capital
        Hellmann & Puri (2002)

        VCs add networks, governance, hires, and signalling — sometimes the real reason to raise.

        The Compass replaces pattern-matching with structured reasoning. What it gives you is a directional reading. What you do with that reading is still entirely yours.
